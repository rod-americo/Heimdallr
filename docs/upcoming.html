Heimdallr Upcoming Roadmap

Planned capabilities for Heimdallr's radiology preprocessing ecosystem, organized to protect delivery quality, clinical safety, and governance as the platform scales.

How to Read This Page

  • Backlog table: what is planned and why.
  • Impact vs Friction: expected value versus production effort.

Rank Legend

  • Rank is execution order, not model sophistication.
  • Ranks 1-6 are the first implementation tranche for new readers and contributors.
  • Lower rank number means higher delivery priority in this roadmap.

Pillar Codes (A-G)

  • A: Logistics Automation and Smart Prefetch
  • B: Workflow Orchestration and Clinical Triage
  • C: Opportunistic Quantification and Precision Triage
  • D: LLM/VLM Reporting Copilot
  • E: De-identification and API Security
  • F: Patient Navigation and Closing the Loop
  • G: Disruptive and Long-Term R&D

Purpose

  • Align engineering, clinical, and governance priorities.
  • Prevent rushed releases in high-risk clinical workflows.
  • Keep scope explicit for contributors and partners.
  • Turn strategy into auditable implementation steps.

Working Definitions

  • Friction (1-10): implementation-in-production effort across integration, data quality, validation, compliance/governance, and operational change.
  • Impact (1-10): expected clinical and operational value once adopted in real workflows.
  • Navigation: active monitoring by a service team that owns each flagged patient until closure.

Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

  1. Human-in-the-loop first: AI prioritizes and drafts; clinicians decide.
  2. Privacy by design: de-identification and least-privilege are mandatory for any external AI call.
  3. Workflow over isolated models: optimize end-to-end throughput and follow-up completion.
  4. Measured delivery: every module must ship with KPIs, logs, and rollback.
  5. Reproducibility: deterministic outputs for the same input/version set.

Navigation Monitoring Criteria

Urology

  • Renal stones >= 0.7 cm
  • Simple renal cysts > 10 cm
  • Bosniak III/IV cysts
  • Solid renal nodules (any size)
  • Solid adrenal nodules > 3 cm
  • Solid ureteral nodules / ureteral stones / UPJ stenosis
  • Solid or vegetative bladder lesions
  • Prostate findings: PI-RADS 3-5, or volume > 70 g, or > 40 g with outlet obstruction signs
  • Solid testicular lesions
  • If neoplasm is suspected, also route to Oncology Navigation

General Surgery + Oncology

  • General Surgery: cholelithiasis and abdominal wall hernias
  • Oncology: route all suspicious/potentially neoplastic findings in ER and inpatient populations
  • Keep owner, due date, status, and escalation until closure

Gynecology

  • Solid/predominantly solid ovarian masses
  • Complex cystic adnexal lesions with suspicious components
  • Ascites, peritoneal/omental/retroperitoneal implants
  • Pelvic/retroperitoneal suspicious adenopathy
  • Adnexal O-RADS 4
  • Suspicious endometrial/uterine/cervical findings
  • Findings suspicious for endometriosis requiring structured follow-up

Top Risk Register

  1. Regulatory variance across LGPD/GDPR/HIPAA-like environments.
  2. PACS/RIS/EHR integration heterogeneity and legacy constraints.
  3. Model/API drift and vendor-side behavior changes.
  4. GPU capacity bottlenecks for segmentation-heavy modules.
  5. Clinical adoption risk without explicit governance ownership.

Platform Definition of Done

  • Functional implementation with rollback path.
  • Clinical safety boundaries and reviewer accountability.
  • Security/privacy controls aligned with data class.
  • Monitoring dashboards, logs, and runbooks.
  • Validation datasets and acceptance metrics documented.
